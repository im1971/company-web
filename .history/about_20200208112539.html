“Programming Maestro” is targeted to inspire the young children and train them so that they can be skilled enough to empower themselves going beyond all the social and economic barriers as well as they can train others to empower thyself in the same way. We will create a platform for the future generation to be efficient on programming.

The world is racing towards the 5th generation of technological development. The people has to adopt new technologies as well as new techniques to keep pace with the current wave of development. This craving for technological development requires a very skilled and hardworking manpower to accelerate the process. Moreover, the traditional jobs are not sufficient to fulfil the economic sovereignty.

The ILO (International Labour Organization) expresses its concern over high youth unemployment rate. The ILO says, one in five young people under 25 are jobless and have no skills. It warns the compromises of the future employment prospects.

If a developing country like Bangladesh wants to embrace the economic autarky, it’s high time to emancipate fledglings of its population by emphasizing on technological competency. The “Programming Maestro” is an initiative to encourage young people and children in programming as well as in technological competencies.

Programming is a universal language that requires continuous effort and practice to be an expert programmer besides having some basic knowledge and that makes it unique.

We have segregated the whole learning process into 2 basic steps that involves-

1. Learn the basic knowledge of programming

2. Continuous practice

These two steps are very much continuing and interconnected. We will follow a series of activities to properly execute the steps.

First step: Learn the basic knowledge of programming

1. Future Coder Selection
2. Train the Future Coder
3. Programming Maestro Selection
4. Final Coding Challenge

At first, we will run campaigns in schools and colleges to make the children be acquainted with the useful aspects of computer programming we will convince them and its make them understandable to it’s benefits.

Secondly, we will conduct a test called "Future Coder Selection". Through this test, we will see who really wants to learn and who is capable to learn "Computer Programming."

Third, we will select 200 students from the above test. We will train these 20 people about "Computer Programming" for six days (four hours per day) through an event called "Future Coder". we will teach them practically what "Computer Programming" is and why we should do it.

Fourth, we will select 20 "Programming Maestro" from these 200 students depending on the examination and understanding. We will organize a "Programming Competition" by dividing them into 7 groups.

In the end, just these 6 days of training are not enough to teach "Computer Programming." Therefore, we will set up an institute with sophisticated computer and technology where the children can learn free "Computer Programming" for 6 days in a week.

Second step: Continuous Practice

In the second step, we will launch a programming lab with latest digital facilities for practice programming regularly. Where students will be able to learn basic to advanced level programming. Where the topper from the national and international programming competition will play the role of teachers.
